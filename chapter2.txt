The Seven Wonders of the Ancient World are remarkable constructions of classical antiquity. They were originally listed by ancient Greek travelers and scholars as the most impressive man-made structures known to them. The traditional list includes:

1. The Great Pyramid of Giza (Egypt) – The only surviving wonder, built as a tomb for Pharaoh Khufu.
2. The Hanging Gardens of Babylon (Iraq) – Legendary terraced gardens, whose existence is debated.
3. The Statue of Zeus at Olympia (Greece) – A massive gold and ivory statue honoring the king of the Greek gods.
4. The Temple of Artemis at Ephesus (Turkey) – A grand temple dedicated to the goddess Artemis.
5. The Mausoleum at Halicarnassus (Turkey) – An elaborate tomb built for Mausolus, a Persian satrap.
6. The Colossus of Rhodes (Greece) – A giant bronze statue of the sun god Helios, once standing at the harbor entrance.
7. The Lighthouse of Alexandria (Egypt) – Also known as the Pharos of Alexandria, it guided sailors safely to port.

These wonders symbolize the ingenuity and artistic achievement of ancient civilizations.
