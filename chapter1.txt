The Taj Mahal is a renowned mausoleum located in Agra, India. Built between 1632 and 1653 by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, it was constructed in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. The structure is celebrated for its stunning white marble architecture, intricate carvings, and symmetrical gardens. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Taj Mahal attracts millions of visitors each year and is considered one of the finest examples of Mughal architecture, blending elements from Islamic, Persian, and Indian styles. Its beauty and historical significance make it an enduring symbol of love and artistry.
